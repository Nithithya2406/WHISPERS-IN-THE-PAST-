Leonardo Da Vinci

His Biodata

Leonardo da Vinci (1452â€“1519) was an Italian polymath whose talents spanned art, science, engineering, and anatomy. Born in Vinci, Tuscany, he was the illegitimate son of a notary and a peasant woman. At around 14, he began an apprenticeship with the renowned artist Andrea del Verrocchio in Florence, where he honed his skills in painting, sculpture, and mechanical design

His History

Leonardo's most celebrated works include the "Mona Lisa" and "The Last Supper," masterpieces that showcase his mastery of light, perspective, and human emotion. Beyond painting, he filled numerous notebooks with sketches and ideas for inventions, anatomical studies, and observations on nature. His designs anticipated modern devices like the helicopter and the bicycle, though many were never built in his lifetime .
