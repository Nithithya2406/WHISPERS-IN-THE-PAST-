Mona Lisa

The Mona Lisa, painted by Leonardo da Vinci, is a world-renowned portrait believed to depict Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a Florentine merchant. Da Vinci worked on the painting between 1503 and 1517, and it is now housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris. Its fame is attributed to its enigmatic smile, innovative artistic techniques like sfumato, and its historical journey, including a theft in 1911 that significantly boosted its popularity.
